Communities

Community Management

Running online communities isn't easy and every community is unique. I know from my seven years of experience running my own community. If you're new to community management, I can't recommend more reading Art of Community. It has loads of indispensable advice from a seasoned veteran in the industry. If you need help or advice with your own community, we're here to help. As a community leader or manager, I'm sure you're passionate about providing the best user experience and features to your community.

Social Graph

Our social networks use a follower model for relationships. When a user follows another, they become a leader. Two users that follow each other are consider mutual. This allows users to control what content is available, such as access to their pictures or statues, to their leaders, followers and mutual relationships. As well, it customizes their dashboard so they can stay up to date on their leaders' activities.

@Mentions, #Hashtags and Trending, Oh my!

We do mentions and hash-tagging right. A lot of other frameworks provide tagging fields which isn't a nice user experience. Instead any #hashtags in a post's body are automatically processed. Trending hashtags are displayed on the dashboard and posts are searchable by hashtag. @Mentions allow users to quickly and easily contact each other, with on-site and email notifications.

Social Apps

We include several important social apps along with the base platform. These include groups, photos, todos, topics and (wiki) pages. As well, there's a subscription app that allows you to sell memberships to your community and a social invite app that allows your users to invite their friends. On top of that, the platform is extremely extendable and custom social apps can be created that incorporate all of the platforms features and integrate seamlessly.
